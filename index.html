Welcome Back Guests And Busters!
Welcome to the website for our First Lego League robotics team called the “Kragle Busters!” This is our second year competing in the First Lego League robotics competition for kids 9 - 14. In early fall, FLL releases a Challenge, which is based on a real-world scientific topic. This year’s topic is going to have something to do with “Waste Management.” The kids will be given a broad topic about Waste Management and will have to find a problem that they can solve (for example something like finding a way to encourage people to compost kitchen waste, or maybe building a better recycling bin). The variety of problems the thousands of different teams worldwide choose to solve each year is amazing. Each Challenge has three parts: the Robot Game, the Project, and the FLL Core Values. Teams of up to ten kids with at least two adult coaches participate in the Challenge by programming an autonomous robot to score points on a themed playing field (Robot Game), and developing a solution to a problem they have identified (Project), all guided by the First Lego League Core Values. Teams then attend an official tournament hosted by one of our FIRST LEGO League Partners. Follow along on our Blog (hopefully updated weekly!) as we progress through the season, meet our team members, or look at our calendar to see what we’re doing this week and into the future. The most important part of the First Lego League Core Values is to HAVE FUN!! While FLL is incredibly fun and educational activity, it is very expensive. Between buying a robot kit, the game board and pieces, putting together the project, building a product prototype, and promoting their team, the end cost is around $2500 per team. Our team has been partially sponsored both last year and this year by the Scrum Alliance for half of that amount. We are a small team, so covering the entire $1,250 ourselves is onerous for the families involved. Please donate if you can!
